What to Delegate

  • Interesting
  • Development
  • Where the context is known

_Got_ to be of interest to the person your delegating to _Should develop the person _Must_ be where the context is known

Danger

  • No context
  • Answer you can not live with
  • No interest or boring

If people dont have the right context, they may make the 'wrong' answer You delegate Authority, but keep responsibility So you have to live with the decision. You be accused of being lazy if it is boring

Context

  • Does the person know ?
  • How, preparation ?
  • Strategy, intent - not how

Communication, Communication, Communication. What are we trying to achieve ? Need to have done the prparation before hand. You not communicating how to do it or even what to do but your intent. e.g. To lower cost per installation, not what to cut or how to get the cut.

Live with it

  • Think of all the outcomes
  • Trust the delegated person
  • Roll with it

If you have the _Intent_ correct; then this should not be a problem.

Avoid Boring

  • Not the rubbish
  • Might have to sell it
  • Communication is key

Reason for delegatign something should not be it is boring.

Mistaken Dangers

  • Too difficult
  • Not authorised
  • Not competent

Too difficult; Good -> Interesting, Development You are passing your Authority (but not responsibility) to them You never going to grow people if you assuem not competent

Do, Check, Go

  • Still authorising

Do, Inform, Go

  • Less complex
  • Giving authority
  • Still responsible

Just Do it

  • Giving authority
  • Still responsible
  • Builds trust, if not seen as lazy
